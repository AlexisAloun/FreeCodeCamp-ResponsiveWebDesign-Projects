Dr. Norman Borlaug

An American Agricultural Scientist

Accomplishments:

  • 1965 - In 1965, after extensive testing, Borlaug's team, under Anderson, began its effort by importing about 450 tons of Lerma Rojo and Sonora 64 semi-dwarf seed varieties: 250 tons went to Pakistan and 200 to India.
  • 1968 - Borlaug received what he considered an especially satisfying tribute when the people of Ciudad Obregón, where some of his earliest experiments were undertaken, named a street after him. Also in that year, he became a member of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences.
  • 1970 - He was given an honorary doctorate by the Agricultural University of Norway. He also was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize by the Norwegian Nobel Committee "for his contributions to the 'green revolution' that was having such an impact on food production particularly in Asia and in Latin America.
  • 1971 - Borlaug received the American Academy of Achievement's Golden Plate Award.
  • 1974 - In 1974, he was awarded a Peace Medal (in the form of a dove, carrying a wheat ear in its beak) by Haryana Agricultural University, Hisar, India.
  • 1975 - In 1975, he was named a Distinguished Fellow of the Iowa Academy of Science.
  • 1980 - He received the S. Roger Horchow Award for Greatest Public Service by a Private Citizen, an award given out annually by Jefferson Awards. He was elected honorary member of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences, too.
  • 1986 - Borlaug was inducted into the Scandinavian-American Hall of Fame during Norsk Høstfest.
  • 1997 - Norman Borlaug on World Hunger written by Norman Borlaug was first published in 1997.
  • 2005 - As of January 2004, Borlaug had received 49 honorary degrees from as many universities, in 18 countries, the most recent from Dartmouth College on June 12, 2005, and was a foreign or honorary member of 22 international Academies of Sciences.
  • 2006 - In August 2006, Dr. Leon Hesser published The Man Who Fed the World: Nobel Peace Prize Laureate Norman Borlaug and His Battle to End World Hunger, an account of Borlaug's life and work.
